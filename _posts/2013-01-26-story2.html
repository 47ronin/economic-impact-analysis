Citizens
re-imagining
their urban landscape

streetmix.net
‹ ›


Urban planners typically solicit feedback from residents in community meetings, but time and space constraints make these meetings a limited channel often dominated by a vocal minority. A cross-city team of 2013 Fellows built Streetmix to make a better interface for participatory planning by engaging the community to re-envision their neighborhoods. It’s fun, easy to use, and allows for flexible creativity - and the result is wildly popular. Since the beta launch in August 2013, 2300+ registered users have created 120,000+ streets, “remixing” the urban landscape in Seattle, DC, New Jersey, and cities around the world. The tool continues to be improved, and a custom Tumblr theme allow planners to juxtapose the current and proposed street plan with community ideas.








"In the six months since Streetmix went live, groups have used it to campaign against highway widening in Iowa, champion new buses in New Mexico, and draw attention to bike safety issues in Seattle."
