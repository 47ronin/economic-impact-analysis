2011

In our first year we worked with three Fellowship partner cities.

 2012

In 2012 we partnered with 8 cities for the Fellowship, launched the Brigade, and began a pilot of the Peer Network.

 2013

In 2013 our network grew steadily, with Fellowship teams in 9 cities and counties, 30 Brigade chapters, and staff from over 50 cities joining the Peer Network.

 Institutionalizing Innovation

By 2013 over 13 cities around the country had created offices or high-level roles focused on innovation (i.e. Office of New Urban Mechanics, Chief Innovation Officer). These serve as ongoing spaces for experimentation, building new skill sets, opening data, engaging the community, and demonstrating new ways of leveraging technology that can serve citizens better and save money.

 Community Events

The network participated in two major nationwide events: Code Across America and National Day of Civic Hacking. During these coordinated weekends, citizens and governments convened at dozens of events to build beta apps, liberate datasets and hold write-a-thons. Nine brigade members were then honored with an invitation to a White House Champions of Change celebrating the National Day of Civic Hacking.
