Hear their stories:
giving a year to public service

Amir Reavis-Bey

Partner City: New Orleans

Amir spent more than seven years working as an investment banking technologist in New York City, developing an expertise in systems integration, and has a BS in Computer Science from North Carolina State University. As a CfA Fellow he partnered with the city of New Orleans to develop applications focusing on vacancy and quality of life issues. At the end of their Fellowship year, Amir and the rest of the New Orleans team founded a start-up to sustain their app, BlightStatus, and develop new products. As co-founder of this new venture he's now leading client on-boarding and integration, quality assurance, and technical support.

Emily Wright Moore

Partner City: Austin

Emily Wright is a designer and graphic artist focused on usability and visual storytelling. She has worked for several Bay Area groups, including Banned by the Bay, Red Ink Studios, and Intersection for the Arts, where she researched and co-designed Golden Rules as part of the Prison Project. As part of the Austin CfA team, Emily built Prepared.ly, a tool to help citizens prepare for wildfires. In 2013 she’s joining Recovers, one of the Code for America Accelerator’s 7 civic start-ups, as Chief Creative Officer.

Jesse Bounds

Partner City: Chicago

Jesse Bounds is a software engineer with a decade of experience architecting and developing successful systems using a variety of languages. He was the lead developer on Fluent, the first highly optimized news aggregator and reader for the iPhone. As a CfA Fellow, he was part of the Chicago team that built 311 Labs, which includes four tools to open 311 data to use. He is now an engineer at Pivotal Labs in San Francisco.

Sheba Najmi

Partner City: Honolulu

Sheba Najmi was a lead designer for Yahoo! Mail, the company’s flagship service with over 260 million users, and co-founded a startup focused on the oft-overlooked baby boomer and senior citizen audiences. She has also worked as a television news anchor in Pakistan, interviewing and reporting from both the streets and the studio. Sheba earned MS and BS degrees in Symbolic Systems from Stanford University. As a CfA fellow, she was the Project Lead on Honolulu Answers.
